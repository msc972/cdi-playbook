Compartmentalized Digital Identity

Private is the only compartment sealed on every axis. Inside the Main profile, Citizen, Commerce, and Social are separated by disclosure discipline, not hard walls: they share the main VPN and functional-browser fingerprint, and Commerce and Social also share the Bulk vault and the everything-else SIM. Root of trust

Hardware key ×2

phishing-resistant 2FA

  • Two PIN-set keys, enrolled alike, stored apart
  • Guards the Core vault + crown-jewel logins
  • Paper recovery in a safe (root identity only)

Authenticator app

offline, no cloud account

  • Holds the Core vault's 2FA (cross-2FA)
  • Manual backup to a second device

Core password manager

the personal-side seal

  • Unlocked by hardware key + authenticator
  • Holds Citizen logins only

Two SIMs

registered numbers, split by exposure

  • SIM A → Citizen (bank, eID, 2FA)
  • SIM B → Commerce / everything-else

The disclosure ladder

reveal only what each merchant forces

  • Named delivery: real name + address, virtual card, alias email
  • Paid-pseudonymous: virtual card, parcel locker, alias
  • Alias-only: burner email, no ID, no payment
  • Ignore: disposable alias, fake info

The real invariant: no attribute crosses between the Main side, the Private side, and Work. Within Main, Citizen, Commerce, and Social are separated by disclosure discipline, not walls, so they can share the Bulk vault, the everything-else SIM, and the main VPN; only the Private profile is sealed on every axis (own vault, browser, egress, and VPN or Tor). Work is a fourth, employer-controlled profile, left out of this picture because it is not yours to design.
